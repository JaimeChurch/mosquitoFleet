Mosquitos?

A popular myth tells of a Seattle newspaper man, looking over Elliot Bay at rush hour, and noting that the traffic on the water resembled a “fleet of mosquitos.” In fact, the term was in use long before, originating somewhere in Europe. It appears in the Oxford English Dictionary with a citation from 1804:

“Man and victual the Musquitoe Fleet (as Wit in his wantonness has described it).”

- Joshua Larwood, in “No Gunboats or No Peace!”
